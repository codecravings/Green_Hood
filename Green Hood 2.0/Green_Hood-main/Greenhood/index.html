Taking care of plants...

Water your plants regularly

Title description, Dec 7,2017

Keep the soil moist Either the soil is too dry or overwatered, it can be an obstruction in the growth of the plant. In some cases, less or overwatering the plant also destroys it. Plants which have lush, thick leaves need more water than plants with waxy or leathery leaves. If you see mould formations on the surface of the soil or the water is standing at the bottom of the container that means the plant has been overwatered. Give water when the soil of plant turns lighter in colour or seems cracked. Standing water can kill plants. If you see standing water in or under the pot, then pour it out.

Taking care of your indoor plant

Title description, sep 2,2017

Choose an area in your house that receives proper sunlight Plants need sunlight to prepare food by the process of photosynthesis. quality, time, and intensity of sunlight affect a plant's growth. Do not put the plant under direct sunlight. Keep it them in a well-lit room which gives them enough light. Fluorescent lights can also work in place of sunlight for some plants. Flowering plants need 12-16 hours of light per day. Foliage plants need 14-16 hours of light per day.

Avoid moving your plants around

Title description, sep 2,2017

Plants also adapt themselves to their surroundings with time, therefore it is advised to not move them around a lot. not move your plant to entirely different temperature zone darker to the lighter area. In case you wish to move the plant, slowly try to adapt it to a new place by taking it there for an hour a day in the beginning. Keep on increasing the time it till it adapts fully.

Increase the humidity in the room

Title description, sep 2,2017

Dry air can harm some plants as most plants require humidity, especially tropic plants. You can get a room humidifier from the nearby store with a cool mist, and place it close enough to provide moisture in the air to the plant. Make sure it does not create any foliage or flowers wet. can also go for a cheaper alternative to the humidifier. Filled a tray with pebbles. Put water just up and below the tops of the pebbles. The evaporating water will cause humidity in the room. You can also use a spray bottle with distilled water, and drizzle over the plants to provide the extra moisture. Wilting browned leaves, and flower buds are indication signs that your plant is suffering from low humidity. You can also keep your plants close to each other to increase humidity.

Fill your pot with fertilizer

Title description, sep 2,2017

Most of the houseplants survive in balanced 10-10-10 fertilizer. House plants derive all the nutrients from potting soils and fertilizers to survive. Therefore you need to repot the plant and add new nutrients to the soil, otherwise, the plant will die with the course of time. First is nitrogen, second phosphorus, and the third is potassium. You can remember it as N-P-K. A flowering plant requires a fertilizer that is high in Potassium. Whereas a foliage plant requires a fertilizer or potting soil rich in Nitrogen. Cacti or succulents can survive in a harsh, desert-like environment. They require a special potting mix specially designed to drain water efficiently. You can also have a pot with plenty of holes on the bottom. These prevent plants from holding too much moisture in the soil, which can cause their death.

Prune your plant regularly

Title description, sep 2,2017

Certain plants require their roots to be pruned at regular intervals, therefore it becomes essential to know about your plant and its requirements. A plant which is not pruned can outgrow the pot or vase. By pruning your plant regularly, you will keep it healthy, and prevent to replant. Shave off dead branches or stems as they tend to attract bugs. Do not put tea or coffee into your houseplant Putting coffee or tea into your house plant will attract flies that can eat t your indoor plant. Sugars also serve as a breeding ground for such insects.
